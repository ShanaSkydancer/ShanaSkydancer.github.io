Historical places in the V and A Waterfront

    Clock Tower

  • The Clock Tower has an old tidal-gauge mechanism on the bottom floor, which was used to check tide levels.
  • The clock mechanism can be found on the top floor and thanks to the restoration of this national monument in 1997, the original clock is still in use today.
  • The Clock Tower is the symbol of Cape Town’s seafaring history.

    Swing Bridge

  • The Swing Bridge controls the opening of the blue Bascule Bridge.
  • The Swing Bridge was designed and constructed in 1997.
  • The V and A Marina is accessed from Granger Bay through the Victoria and the Alfred Basins under the Swing Bridge.

    Union Castle Building

  • Castle Building designed in 1919 by the firm of architects owned by Sir Herbert Baker.
  • The Union Steamship Company and the Castle Line both ran monthly mail ships between Britain and South Africa in the late 19th century.
  • The last Union Castle ship to sail to England with the mail was the Windsor Castle in 1977.
